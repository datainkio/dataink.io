Digital Transformation for Public Health

Ensuring public health through user-friendly design, innovative research, and a robust digital presence during the pandemic.

This case study presents a comprehensive analysis of the digital transformation of the United States Department of Agriculture’s Food Safety Inspection Service (FSIS). Undertaken to enhance public health and food safety, the project focused on revamping FSIS's digital presence to ensure user-friendliness and accessibility of vital information. The study highlights the challenges faced due to COVID-19 restrictions and the team's commitment to human-centered design, which led to the successful overhaul of FSIS's website. Key elements of this transformation included an empirical design approach, innovative research methodologies, and a strong focus on rapid prototyping and iterative design. The redesigned website now serves as a critical resource for consumers, inspectors, and policymakers, offering a balanced mix of educational resources and specific information tailored to Subject Matter Experts (SMEs). This study underscores the importance of collaborative approaches, user feedback, and data-driven decision-making in creating effective digital solutions. The outcome of the project is a testament to the life-saving potential of well-designed digital platforms in the public health sector. Body This case study outlines the impactful role of experience design in enhancing the digital presence of the USDA’s Food Safety Inspection Service (FSIS), a crucial entity for ensuring public health and food safety. The key aspects of this project include: 1. **Digital Transformation for Public Health:** The project aimed to revamp FSIS’s digital presence, crucial for safeguarding public health by ensuring food safety. This involved creating a user-friendly and informative website to build public trust and aid policy formulation. 2. **Human-Centered Design Amidst Challenges:** Despite the limitations imposed by COVID restrictions, the team maintained a strong focus on human-centered research and design, adapting to remote methodologies without losing sight of user needs. 3. **Widespread Reliance on the New Site:** Post-redesign, the FSIS website became a vital source of information for a broad spectrum of users including consumers, inspectors, and policymakers, particularly for insights into food safety science and practices. 4. **Empirical Design and Content Strategy:** The design process was empirically validated and aligned with a content strategy that bolstered public confidence in the government's role in preventing food-borne illnesses. 5. **Innovative Research and Testing Methods:** The team employed a mix of desktop research, fieldwork, and innovative methods like artifact journeys and market data analysis to enhance their understanding of food safety practices and the diverse audiences FSIS serves. 6. **Connecting Audiences Through HAACP Analysis:** The Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HAACP) plan emerged as a key intersecting artifact among various stakeholders, guiding the research and contextual inquiry. 7. **Information Architecture and Audience Needs:** The team's landscape analysis and audience research led to an information architecture that balanced educational resources for the general public with specific information for Subject Matter Experts (SMEs). 8. **Rapid Prototyping and Iterative Design:** Emphasizing rapid prototyping, the team created tangible prototypes for iterative feedback, ranging from Drupal site mockups to paper prototypes of the homepage. 9. **Collaborative Approach and User Feedback:** Collaboration and user feedback were integral, with early sketches and paper prototyping sessions involving clients, stakeholders, and the design team to establish a shared understanding of the project goals. 10. **Data-Driven Decision Making:** The team relied on quantitative research, web analytics, and testing to inform the content strategy and refine the information architecture. **Successful Outcome and Continued Growth:** The redesigned fsis.usda.gov has seen growing usage and value, providing crucial information on food safety, policy, and inspection. The project is viewed as a life-saving endeavor due to its significant impact on public health and food safety.
