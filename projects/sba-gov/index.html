Description

Originally, SBA.gov served as a vast repository of information and links, which many users found confusing and difficult to navigate. The site also did not comply with federal accessibility guidelines, making it inaccessible for a significant portion of potential users. This led to a high bounce rate, with 52% of visitors leaving the site from the landing page without further engagement. Our task was to understand our audience better and tailor the site to meet their specific needs, creating a more user-centric online environment. We initiated our redesign process by conducting extensive customer research, gathering valuable data, and building detailed personas and user stories that reflected the real needs of actual users. This research informed our content strategy and helped us refine the SBA's brand positioning and integrate a strategic social media plan. The outcome was a user-friendly, accessible website with clear, plain language and a Spanish-language version, all designed with direct input from our target users. This new site effectively supports hundreds of thousands of entrepreneurs and small business owners monthly, providing them with essential tools to start, grow, and sustain their businesses. Our design strategy was intensely human-centered. We engaged in thorough discovery sessions with the SBA team and conducted diverse forms of user research. This comprehensive approach helped us develop accurate customer personas and journey maps, which guided our adjustments to the site's content to better meet user needs. Our efforts led to an extraordinary increase in site traffic and user engagement. In the first half of 2020, the site saw an 801% increase in visitor numbers year-over-year, demonstrating the effectiveness of our user-centered design and content strategy. By focusing on the needs and experiences of SBA.gov's users, we transformed the site into a vital resource that not only meets federal standards but also resonates with and empowers its users.
