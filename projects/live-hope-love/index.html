The Client

The Pulitzer Center on Crisis Reporting is a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting in-depth, high-impact journalism that addresses critical global issues often underreported in mainstream media. Founded in 2006, the Pulitzer Center empowers journalists to cover pressing crises and promotes public engagement through innovative storytelling and educational initiatives.

Innovative Storytelling

Embracing new media and technology, the Pulitzer Center promotes innovative storytelling techniques, including multimedia presentations, virtual reality experiences, and interactive websites. These approaches make complex issues more accessible and engaging for diverse audiences.

Design and Interactivity

We crafted an interactive experience that respected and amplified the content. The design focused on allowing the poetry, images, and stories to speak for themselves without overwhelming the viewer.

Multimedia Integration

The website featured a synthesis of various media forms—vivid video interviews, extraordinary photographs, haunting music, and powerful poetry—all inspired by Dawes’ journey.

Incorporating the Author's Voice

The site featured recordings of Dawes reciting his poems, adding a personal and authentic dimension to the user experience.

Voices of the People

The production team returned to Jamaica to record the voices of individuals featured in Dawes’ poems, lending authenticity and depth to the narrative.

Photography's Role

Josh Cogan’s photography played a critical role in amplifying the sentiment of the poems while maintaining focus on the poetry itself.
