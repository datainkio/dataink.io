Building Transparency into Federal Decision-Making

Collaborated with the General Services Administration (GSA) and the White House to increase transparency into the decisions behind $4.2B in waivers and exceptions to import and export policies.

View

The website for the White House's Made in America Office (MIAO) provided transparency into the U.S. government's procurement processes.

The Challenge

The initiative was driven by an executive order issued on January 25, 2021, by President Biden, emphasizing the federal government's commitment to buying American-made goods. The establishment of the MIAO was a critical part of this order, tasked with reviewing waiver requests from Made in America laws.

The Solution

My team developed a digital tool for MIAO to manage waiver requests efficiently, focusing on clarity, consistency, and transparency across federal agencies. The website aimed to offer U.S. producers and small businesses insight into federal contracting opportunities, supporting the wider goal of boosting American manufacturing. The public database to provided comprehensive visibility on waivers, extending from submission to approval or rejection, thereby enhancing transparency and consistency.

Understanding User Needs through User Stories

User stories were developed for three key user groups: Contracting Officers, MIAO Reviewers, and Federal Contractors. These stories helped to distill the essential problems each group faced in the context of waiver requests.

Prioritizing User Stories

User stories focused on common tasks like specifying correct codes for waiver requests and ensuring the validity of these codes, critical for the smooth processing of waivers.

Agile HCD

The project was grounded in human-centered research and design, maintaining an agile and responsive approach to rapidly adapt to changing requirements.
