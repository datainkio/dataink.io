CarePlex

IBM's CarePlex, developed with Boston Children’s Hospital, leverages Watson AI to enhance global healthcare by providing dynamic medical records, personalized care, and a secure space for medical collaboration.

This case study explores the development of IBM's CarePlex, an innovative healthcare platform utilizing Watson’s artificial intelligence to enhance the medical care process for clinicians, patients, and caregivers. The project, a collaboration between IBM and Boston Children’s Hospital, aimed to integrate cognitive computing into healthcare to provide dynamic medical records and personalized care recommendations. CarePlex emerged as a cloud-based SaaS solution that rapidly connected medical professionals worldwide. Its natural language interface facilitated easy adoption across different regions, requiring minimal training. The platform supported a global community, enhancing medical practices with AI-driven insights. The platform's design and technology bridged gaps between medical professionals, creating a collaborative environment for a global community. CarePlex provided a secure space for sharing second opinions, discussing cases, and accessing a wide range of resources. This case study demonstrates the potential of AI in transforming healthcare delivery and decision-making processes. By integrating cognitive computing with practical, user-centric design, CarePlex exemplified how technology can address complex challenges in healthcare, offering personalized, data-driven support to improve patient outcomes. CarePlex uses IBM’s Watson predictive analytics technology to create a dynamic medical record for patients. Targeting clinicians, patients, and caregivers, the Watson technology offers guidance for medical treatment as it draws from the individual patient’s record as well as aggregated knowledge from entire populations. Physicians, patients, and caregivers can even see a list of experts who can provide a second opinion, and connect with these individuals through the secure CarePlex community. My task was to implement Watson for use in professional medical practice. In order to be valuable in a clinical environment, a system has to be able to distill the huge corpus of information that comprises a particular patient’s complex clinical situation (including symptoms, condition, drugs, therapies, and treatments). The challenge was to present this clinical data in a way that provides insight into the underlying logic behind Watson’s recommendations while ensuring that the most salient points remain clear and accessible.

Design Challenges

The primary challenge was distilling extensive clinical data into an accessible format that highlighted the most relevant information while providing insight into Watson’s reasoning. The system needed to be intuitive and efficient, reducing cognitive load for users in high-stress environments.

Natural Language Interaction

A key feature of CarePlex was its reliance on natural language processing for interaction. This approach allowed users to input and receive information conversationally, streamlining the user experience and focusing attention on critical care decisions.

Transparency & Verifiability

CarePlex emphasized transparency in Watson’s predictive analytics, offering visualizations of how conclusions were drawn. This feature, coupled with verifiable resources like journal articles and expert connections, bolstered trust in the system’s recommendations. Visualization of Watson’s predictive analytics provided insight into how it drew its conclusions. Doctors could assess an individual’s needs and select interventions with greater confidence and accuracy. Automatic recommendations of potential resources to supplement and/or clarify Watson’s recommendations, including journal articles, instructional videos, tests, and connections to known subject matter experts. Physicians, patients, and caregivers could see a list of experts who could provide a second opinion, and connect with these individuals through the secure CarePlex community. 

Creating a Living Medical History

The platform integrated various data sources, including hospital records, medical reports, and real-time data from sensors, to create a comprehensive electronic medical record. This living document served as a foundation for Watson’s analysis and recommendations.

Bringing Value to Physicians, Patients, and Caregivers

In order to be valuable in a clinical environment, a system has to be able to distill the huge corpus of information that comprises a particular patient’s complex clinical situation (including symptoms, condition, drugs, therapies, and treatments). The challenge was to present this clinical data in a way that provided insight into the underlying logic behind Watson’s recommendations while ensuring that the most salient points remain clear and accessible. To make this happen, I designed an experience mediated through natural language and validated through transparency, with direct access to external resources to verify Watson’s diagnoses and recommendations.
