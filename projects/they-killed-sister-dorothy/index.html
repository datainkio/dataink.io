They Killed Sister Dorothy

Digital media and web design can effectively complement and extend the impact of documentary films, particularly those dealing with critical social and environmental issues.

Bluecadet created the companion website for a documentary film about Sister Dorothy Stang, an environmental and social activist murdered in the Amazon rainforest. The website aimed to complement the film's narrative and deepen public awareness of the environmental and social issues it presented.
As with all of my experiential work, my task was to ensure that the design framed the content without getting in the way of the story. The architecture, navigation, and flow were all designed to keep the user returning to these questions: Who was this woman? Why was she killed? And what will be done about it?

Background of Sister Dorothy Stang

Sister Dorothy was an advocate for rainforest conservation and the rights of peasants in the Amazon. She was tragically murdered in 2005, which brought international attention to the conflict between peasants and wealthy ranchers in the Amazon.

Film Narration and Recognition

The documentary, narrated by Martin Sheen, won awards at the South by Southwest Festival and was pre-selected for the Academy Awards. It played a crucial role in reigniting interest and legal action in Sister Dorothy's case.

Bluecadet’s Challenge

Motivated by social justice, Bluecadet undertook the project to create a companion site for the film, aiming to support the documentary’s social and environmental message.
The main challenge was to balance the portrayal of the horror of Sister Dorothy’s murder with the beauty of the rainforest she fought to protect, without overshadowing the essential information.

Content Strategy and a Cinematic Experience

The website incorporated clips from the film as background for different sections. This design choice effectively conveyed the atmospheric tension of the story, enhancing the user's engagement with the content.
