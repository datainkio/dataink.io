National Museum of Wildlife Art: Inspiring Conservation Through Art

The National Museum of Wildlife Art, situated in the breathtaking landscape of Jackson Hole, Wyoming, stands as a premier institution dedicated to the intersection of art and nature. Overlooking the National Elk Refuge, the museum uniquely blends artistic excellence with wildlife conservation, offering visitors an enriching experience that highlights the beauty and importance of the natural world.

Exceptional Art Collection

The museum boasts an extensive collection of over 5,000 works, spanning from 2500 B.C. to contemporary pieces. The collection includes paintings, sculptures, photography, and mixed media by renowned artists such as Carl Rungius, Robert Bateman, and Georgia O'Keeffe. These artworks capture the essence and diversity of wildlife across different periods and styles.

Engaging Exhibitions

The National Museum of Wildlife Art features a variety of permanent and temporary exhibitions that explore themes of wildlife, natural habitats, and conservation. These exhibitions not only showcase artistic talent but also educate visitors about the importance of preserving wildlife and their environments.

Educational Programs

Committed to fostering a deeper understanding and appreciation of wildlife and art, the museum offers a wide range of educational programs. These include guided tours, art workshops, lectures, and school programs designed for all ages. These programs aim to inspire creativity and environmental stewardship among participants.

Sculpture Trail

The museum’s unique Sculpture Trail is a three-quarter-mile outdoor path that features over 30 wildlife sculptures set against the stunning backdrop of Jackson Hole’s natural scenery. This trail provides an immersive experience where art and nature converge, allowing visitors to appreciate wildlife art in an open-air setting.

Conservation Advocacy

The National Museum of Wildlife Art actively promotes wildlife conservation through its exhibitions, programs, and partnerships. By showcasing the beauty and fragility of wildlife through art, the museum raises awareness and encourages actions that support conservation efforts.

What Makes the National Museum of Wildlife Art Stand Out?

Comprehensive Collection

The museum’s diverse collection of wildlife art spans centuries and includes works from many renowned artists, providing a rich and varied visual experience

Educational Commitment

Through a wide range of programs and workshops, the museum fosters a deeper understanding of wildlife and art, engaging audiences of all ages in conservation efforts

Unique Sculpture Trail

The outdoor Sculpture Trail offers a distinctive way to experience wildlife art, set in the natural beauty of Jackson Hole

Conservation Focus

The museum’s emphasis on wildlife conservation highlights the critical role of art in raising awareness and promoting the protection of natural habitats

Inspirational Setting

Nestled in a stunning natural landscape, the museum provides a serene and inspiring environment that enhances the appreciation of both art and wildlife
