Harvard Housing System: A Vibrant and Supportive Community

The housing system at Harvard University is designed to create a vibrant, inclusive, and supportive living environment that enhances the academic and social experience for students. With a rich tradition and a variety of housing options, Harvard provides a unique residential experience that fosters community, personal growth, and academic success.

House System

Harvardâ€™s renowned House system for undergraduate students is one of its defining features. Each of the twelve Houses serves as a small, close-knit community within the larger university, offering residential, social, and academic support.

Faculty Deans and Tutors

Each House is led by Faculty Deans, resident scholars who provide intellectual and social leadership. They are supported by Resident Tutors and Proctors, who offer academic advising, personal support, and help organize House activities, creating a robust support network for students.

Community and Tradition

The House system fosters a sense of community and tradition through regular events, intramural sports, social activities, and House-specific traditions. This rich cultural environment encourages students to build lasting relationships and engage fully in campus life.
