Stanford Human Rights Clinic: Advancing Justice and Human Dignity

The Stanford Human Rights Clinic (SHRC) is an integral part of Stanford Law School, dedicated to promoting human rights through legal advocacy, research, and education. By combining rigorous academic training with hands-on experience, the clinic empowers students to tackle complex human rights issues and contribute to global justice.

Practical Legal Training

The Stanford Human Rights Clinic offers law students the opportunity to gain practical legal experience by working on real-world human rights cases and projects. Under the supervision of experienced faculty, students engage in advocacy, litigation, and research, honing their skills and preparing for careers in human rights law.

Diverse Human Rights Projects

The clinic addresses a wide range of human rights issues, including but not limited to, refugee and asylum law, transitional justice, gender-based violence, and economic, social, and cultural rights. This diversity allows students to explore various aspects of human rights work and develop a comprehensive understanding of the field.

Advocacy and Impact

The SHRC is committed to making a tangible impact on human rights both locally and globally. Through strategic litigation, policy advocacy, and partnerships with non-governmental organizations, the clinic strives to address systemic injustices and protect the rights of marginalized communities.

Interdisciplinary Approach

The clinic fosters an interdisciplinary approach to human rights, collaborating with other departments and programs at Stanford University. This cross-disciplinary cooperation enriches the clinic’s work and allows for innovative solutions to complex human rights challenges.

Global Engagement

The Stanford Human Rights Clinic operates on a global scale, working on cases and projects that span multiple countries and regions. This international focus provides students with a broad perspective on human rights issues and the opportunity to engage with diverse legal systems and cultures.

Commitment to Education

The SHRC is dedicated to educating the next generation of human rights advocates. By integrating theory and practice, the clinic provides students with a robust educational experience that emphasizes critical thinking, ethical advocacy, and a deep commitment to justice.
